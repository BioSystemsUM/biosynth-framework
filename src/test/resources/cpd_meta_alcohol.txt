    <comment datatype='string'>&lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Alcohols&quot;&gt;Alcohols&lt;/a&gt; are organic compounds in which the hydroxyl functional group (-OH) is bound to a carbon atom. Alcohols are classified into primary, secondary, and tertiary, based upon the number of carbon atoms connected to the carbon atom that bears the hydroxyl group. The &lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Primary-Alcohols&quot;&gt;primary alcohols&lt;/a&gt; have general formulas RCH&lt;sub&gt;2&lt;/sub&gt;OH; &lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Secondary-Alcohols&quot;&gt;secondary alcohols&lt;/a&gt; are RR'CHOH; and tertiary ones are RR'R&quot;COH, where R, R', and R&quot; stand for alkyl groups.

Alcohols are also classified based on the length of the alkyl groups.

&lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Short-Chain-Alcohols&quot;&gt;Short-chain alcohols&lt;/a&gt; have alkyl chains of 1- 3 carbons.

&lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Medium-Chain-Alcohols&quot;&gt;Medium-chain alcohols&lt;/a&gt; have alkyl chains of 4-7 carbons.

&lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Long-chain-alcohols&quot;&gt;Long-chain alcohols&lt;/a&gt; (also known as fatty alcohols) have alkyl chains of 8-21 carbons.

&lt;a href=&quot;http://biocyc.org/META/new-image?object=Very-Long-Chain-Alcohols&quot;&gt;Very long-chain alcohols&lt;/a&gt; have alkyl chains of 22 carbons or longer.</comment>
